How I did it:

I found a 3D shape of Batman's face made by Mocery on Thingiverse https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:34066/ and  placed it inside a 3 Dimensional box I made in Rhino and exported an .stl file. Next I brought the .stl in to Fusion so I could assign a few toolpaths to its shape. One for a 1/8" Flat End Mill with a 1.5 mm Step Down and another one for a 1/32" Flat End Mill with a 1mm Step Down to refine the shape. Once these were done I exported them both as .gcode and opened them up in Bantam Tools. In Bantam Tools I set the dimensions and placement of my material and then set the placement of my gcode for it's milling session. Once this was ready I hot glued a wax box to the tray inside of the CNC Mill and ran my toolpaths. Then when the wax was cut and I had a shape I poured OOMOO insde of it to make a silicon mold. Once the mold was ready I mixed plaster and poured it in to the Silcon mold and proceeded to cast 4 molds of Batman.

Special Thanks to Andrew Waer and Cindy Feng for helping me resolve my OOMOO issues.
